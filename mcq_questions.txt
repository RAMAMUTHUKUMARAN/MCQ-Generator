Question: Which of the following is the primary function of chlorophyll in photosynthesis?
  A. To absorb light energy
  B. To transport water to the leaves
  C. To release oxygen into the atmosphere
  D. To convert glucose into starch
Answer: A
Explanation: Chlorophyll is a pigment that absorbs light energy, primarily in the red and blue regions of the electromagnetic spectrum. This absorbed light energy is then used to drive the process of photosynthesis.
